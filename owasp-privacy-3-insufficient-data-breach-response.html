    OWASP Top 10 Privacy Risk Countermeasures

    OWASP is the acronym for the Open Web Application Security Project .

    3. Insufficient Data Breach Response

    Risk Description

    Not informing the affected persons (data subjects) about a possible breach or data leak, resulting either from:

    1. Intentional or unintentional events

    2. Failure to remedy the situation by fixing the cause

    3. Not attempting to limit the leaks.

    How to check?

    1. General questions

      1. Is an incident response plan for privacy incidents in place?

      2. Is this plan tested regularly (provide evidence e.g., a test protocol)?

      3. Do you have a Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT) and / or a Privacy Team?

      4. Do you have monitoring for incidents (e.g., SIEM) in place?

    2. If there was a privacy incident, did you

      1. Detect it (timeously)?

      2. Notify relevant parties, including the individuals themselves, in a timely manner?

      3. Protect evidence, remaining data during response / investigation?

    3. Is your incident response

      1. Timely: information is disclosed to affected parties soon enough for them to avoid additional harm?

      2. Honest, accurate and understandable? Organizations that experience a privacy breach have a responsibility to clearly communicate the nature and scope of the breach to those affected.

      3. Established company wide for security breach notifications (policy)?

    Countermeasures

    1. Countermeasures (in advance)

      1. Create and maintain an incident response plan.

      2. Test incident response plan regularly.

      3. Include privacy-related incidents in test.

      4. Establish a Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT).

      5. Establish a Privacy Team.

      6. Continuously monitor for personal data leakage and loss.

    2. Responding to the breach

      1. Validate the breach.

      2. Once a breach has been validated, immediately assign an incident manager to be responsible for the investigation.

      3. Assemble an incident response team.

      4. Determine the scope and composition of the breach (e.g., legislation, confidentiality).

      5. >Notify the data owners.

      6. Determine whether to notify the authorities (situation dependent).

      7. Decide how to investigate the data breach to ensure that the evidence is appropriately handled.

      8. Determine whether notification of affected individuals is appropriate and if so, when and how.

      9. Collect and review any breach response documentation and analyse reports.
